Comment l'aide à la procréation a-t-elle évolué au cours du temps ?



From the 18th century, to solve the problems of infertility, scientists and doctors try to find another solution to reproduce differently than natural reproduction. Indeed in that century, Lazzaro Spallanzani carried out the first artificial insemination with a bitch. It was a success which represented a real progress in the medical field which allowed the development of new techniques.

After that, at the end of the 18th century, John HUNTER carried out the first artificial insemination for a couple. Then, at the beginning of the 1800s, the first artificial insemination took place in France.

Moreover the first artificial insemination with donations of spermatozoons dates to 1884. In the same decade, the chorionic gonadotropin hormone was conceived. This hormone allows the stimulation of ovaries for women, who don’t produce enough oocytes.

In 1972 the technique of sperm freezing arrives in France, as well as centres of studies which store human eggs and sperm (Centres d’Etudes et de Conservation des Oeufs et du Sperme Humain, in French [CECOS]).

In 1978 in the United Kingdom, Louise Brown was born; she is the first child born thanks to the in-vitro fertilisation, followed in France by Amandine in 1982. The children born thanks to this technique are called « test-tube babies ».

Additionally the medically assisted reproduction develops with Zoé’s birth in Australia in 1984 thanks to embryo transfers, then with the first baby born in France in 2004 with this method.

From the year 1992 the in-vitro fertilisation with Intra Cytoplasmic Sperm Injection (ICSI) is set up in Brussels, which allowed Audrey’s birth in 1994, the first child who was conceived thanks to this technique. At the same year, France votes for the first Bioethics laws about medically assisted reproduction, then these laws are reviewed in 2004, then in 2011. They are the laws, which clarify the conditions in which couples can resort to these methods, the laws which regulate the donation of sperm…
