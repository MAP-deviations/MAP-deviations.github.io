How is Medically Assisted Procreation surrounded by the law in the foreign countries ?



In the foreign countries, the legal framework framing medically assisted reproduction can sometimes be more flexible than in France.

To study the legislation of a foreign country about the subject, let's take the example of Belgium: today, in Belgium, the medically assisted reproduction is less controlled than in France. Indeed, each person who wants to become a parent can make the request; consequently that includes the homosexual couples or the unmarried woman. However, only the eighteen years old women minimum are concerned, and the age limit is fixed at forty-five years old. Moreover the infertility of individuals must be diagnosed by a doctor.
The Belgian law adopted in 2006 authorizes the transfer of embryos after the death of one of the member of the couple, contrary to the French law.

Furthermore, given that no legislation forbids the appeal to a “surrogate mother”, it’s in this way allowed. But only an adoption which is done by the “intended mother” can invert the family relationship that the child possesses with the “surrogate mother” at his birth. Moreover, the gamete donation is not anonymous in Belgium; a couple can ask their friends or their family to be entitled to it.

We can also add that Belgium isn’t the only country in the world which allows the surrogate motherhood. Indeed, some countries, in particular the developing countries, don’t frame it; so, it’s allowed. Among them, there are Poland, Russia, Australia, and South Africa.

Other countries, like some states of the United States or India, Brazil, Ukraine, United Kingdom and Canada, introduce legal frames which frames this practice, even if they are still a minority.
Besides, India and the United States, for example, officially allow the remuneration of the “surrogate mothers”.

In the United States, the surrogate motherhood has become a real market. The couples who want to resort to it, can look for the mother who is going to carry their child in catalogues. In some countries, it’s the same for the gamete donation because it don’t respect the principle of anonymity anymore. They let the possibility to choose the donor according to the own criteria of the couple, and the couple can even pay the donor.

Furthermore, many countries such as Sweden, Finland, Spain, the United Kingdom and Holland allow medically assisted reproduction for the homosexual couples.

The rigidity of French laws explains why some couples go to foreign countries to turn to medically assisted procreation, where the criteria are less selective. However the flexibility of laws in foreign countries can sometimes leave out the rigor and the quality of medical treatment. That can bring medical complications on the health of individuals.
